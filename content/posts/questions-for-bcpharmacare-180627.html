What went on behind closed doors?

How BC Pharmacare selects its suppliers is not a very public process. Although the recent extension of insulin pump coverage to adults is good news, the BC government news release and BC Pharmacare’s information page leave many questions unanswered.

There may be good reasons for the selection process being conducted in private, but as the program involves spending approximately $15 million over the next three years, the public has the right to know how and why BC Pharmacare selected its pump suppliers.

BC Pharmacare has two review processes, the:

  • BC Health Technology Review, which reviews clinical technologies, and the
  • Drug Review Process, for pharmaceuticals, through its
    • Health Technology Assessment Committee

Which of these processes was used to select the pump suppliers is not clear. As insulin pumps are a technology, it would seem rational to infer that the first process was used, but as its focus is on hospital-based technologies, perhaps the latter was the case?

Enough speculation! Too many questions remain. Here’s my list so far:

Access to the Program

A person must be recommended to the program by an endocrinologist or a “diabetes specialist”. It’s difficult to get an appointment with an endocrinologist in BC, which limits access.

  • What is the definition of a “diabetes specialist”?
  • Are there any plans to speed up access to endocrinologists and/or specialists?

Selection Process

  • Which review process was followed for insulin pumps?
  • What was the composition of the selection committee?
    • Were there representatives of the user on the committee?
    • Were there representatives of the pump suppliers on the committee?
  • Was the Committee’s deliberations minuted?
    • Are these minutes available to the public?
  • Was there a public consultation process informing the Committee?
    • If not, why not?
    • If so, are the responses available to the public?

Selection Criteria

  • Was this an open process available to all suppliers?
    • Which suppliers applied for consideration?
    • Which devices and suppliers were considered?
  • What were the criteria for selection of pump suppliers?
    • Are these criteria available to the public?
    • Were these criteria weighted in the evaluation?
    • What weight was given to each of the criteria?
    • Was cost the primary consideration on the final selection?
  • How did the various suppliers perform in this evaluation?
    • Is this evaluation available to the public?

Tiered Approach

  • What were the reasons behind adoption of a tiered approach for the final selection?
  • Why were the Omnipod and Medtronic 630G selected as tier1 and tier2 pumps?

Sole Sourcing

  • Why was a lowest-cost, sole-source approach taken in this case?
  • Are other types of device being considered for coverage (e.g. FGM)?

The Contract

  • What is the value and duration of the supply contracts issued?
    • Is this contract available for public inspection?
  • Are there provisions for performance evaluation during the contract period?
    • Are there provisions for renegotiation based on performance?

BC Pharmacare, we await your response.
